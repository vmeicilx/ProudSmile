Invisalign is the number 1 clear aligner therapy in the world. More than 5 million patients have been treated with this technology. You can enjoy straighter teeth in as little as 3 months. What is so great about Invisalign?

Nearly Invisible

Invisalign is virtually invisible. This means you can always smile with confidence, as compared to traditional braces where the wires and metal brackets can be seen.

Removable

Invisalign aligners are made with SmartTrack flexible material, making it super easy to remove and put on, unlike braces wich are fixed. Now you can enjoy your favourite food any time you want.

Lesser Pain

SmartStage and Smart Force technologies ensure there will only be gradual and gentle yet effective teeth movement troughout your treatment, as compared to braces wich may irritate and cause pain.

Lesser Doctor Visits

Unlike braces where you have to visit your doctor every 3-4 weeks, Invisalign frees up your schedule with visits to the clinic only once every 6-8 weeks.

Clearer Timelines

Invisalign treatment allows you to visualize every stage of the treatment, giving you a clearer picture of the treatment duration. Braces can only give you an approximate duration at the beginning of the treatment.

Better Oral Health

Since Invisalign aligners are removable, you can still floss and brush your teeth as per normal whereas bracers may make it difficult for you to clean in between the metal brackets which can result in plaque accumulation and poor oral hygiene.

The Invisalign Experience

Be prepared to be impressed by your Invisalign journey. From your free assessment all the way to your straight smile there’s loads of technology at every stage. The whole process if fully digital and clean. And it’s very fast too. You’ll quickly understand why Invisalign is the number 1 in clear aligner therapy in the world.

Invisalign aligners look similar to clear retainers, teeth-whitening trays, or gum shields. A series of Invisalign aligners are custom-made for you and each aligner will contain the patient identification number (PID number) and the Invisalign logo.

The length of your treatment will depend on your specific needs and will be determined by your doctor. The average treatment for Invisalign Full or Invisalign Teen is about 12 to 18 months and for Invisalign Go it’s between 2 to 5 months, treatment length may vary depending on each individual case and will need to be determined by the doctor.

You should wear each aligner for 20 to 22 hours a day during your treatment for best results and a timely outcome.

You will visit your doctor for a check approximately every 6-8 weeks to see how your treatment is progressing. During your check-ups, your Invisalign-trained doctor will check that the aligners are fitting well and make any adjustments if necessary, more aligners are then provided.

You will usually change your aligners every week. However, please follow your Invisalign-trained doctor’s specific instructions regarding when you need to change your aligners as this may vary between patients.

Results with Invisalign Full usually start showing a few months after starting treatment and for Invisalign Go usually after 4 weeks. You can now download the Invisalign App from the Apple store or Google Play and you can monitor results on your phone.

Yes, most insurances will cover Aligner Therapy if you have orthodontics on your package. Claims are usually made over 2 years. Depending on your rebate we can use that towards a payment plan if you choose to use that to pay for your treatment.

Similar to other orthodontic treatments, the Invisalign aligners may temporarily affect the speech of some people for a day or two. However, as your tongue gets used to having aligners in your mouth, any misarticulation or minor speech impediment caused by the aligners should disappear.

While Invisalign treatment may move your teeth without the pain and anxiety of other straightening methods, some people might experience temporary, minor discomfort for a few days at the beginning of each new stage of treatment. This is normal and is typically described as a feeling of pressure. It's a sign that the Invisalign aligners are sequentially moving your teeth to their final positions. This discomfort usually goes away a couple of days after you insert the new aligner in the series.

The aligners are made of non-toxic, medical-grade plastic and can be worn during pregnancy. If you are concerned, please consult your healthcare professional.
