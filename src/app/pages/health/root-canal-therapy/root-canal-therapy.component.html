THE OFFICE

What to expect?

Root canal therapy is performed under Local Anaesthesia and it involves removing all decay and the damaged pulp from the tooth. After the pulp is removed and the canals are clean and disinfected a filling material is placed inside the tooth to close everything and seal all canals so the bacteria can no longer survive and cause more problems.

Depending on the case a root canal can be done in a single visit, when not associated to an emergency situation. If you do have an emergency and you’re in pain we’ll usually deal with the pain first and then continue the procedure on the next visit.

We use rotary instruments instead of manual, that means your treatment will be faster and more efficient. We also use digital x-rays to monitor the progress of the treatment, all is done right there in our super comfy chair while you watch your favourite Netflix show on our ceiling mounted TV.

What happens
after?

Most of the times when you need a root canal there’s significant destruction on the tooth or severe fracture. Once the root canal is treated and the tooth is saved our next step is to restore the tooth and protect it so it will last a long time in your mouth. This is done with a dental crown.

At Proud Smile we can produce crowns in a single day procedure with the use of digital impressions and a milling machine. And the best part is that you won’t need local anaesthetic anymore because the tooth will no longer have a nerve on it.
