Aesthetic Driven Results

Braces are generally known to treat function and aesthetics. By function we mean improper position of the teeth which could lead to dental problems. And then there’s the aesthetics which will give you a nice straight smile.

At Proud Smile we have a strong focus on aesthetics which means we will go an extra step when it comes to braces. We will use the same principles of aesthetics we use for other cosmetic treatments and we will apply them to your braces, that leads to superior aesthetic results and extreme attention to details. But make no mistake, we will also treat the function with extreme care by following strict orthodontic principles.

Yes, even though we won’t be placing braces on an 8 year old child we can certainly identify early problems at this age, including bad habits. In fact, 8 is the perfect time to start because a few adult teeth are present and we can check for growth problems too such as excessive growth of the lower jaw, not enough development of the upper jaw, crowding, cross bites amongst other problems. If we do identify a problem we can often treat these with removable or fixed appliances and by doing that we will be minimising or even avoiding all together the need for braces in the near future.

  • Twin block - This is an appliance to correct the relationship between the top and bottom jaws. If your child has a large overbite and the lower jaw is not developed enough we can improve and treat that with these types of appliances. The results can be pretty incredible dependable on compliance and your child will benefit from increased confidence at school and better speech capabilities.



  • Cross bite: This is the condition where the bottom teeth are in front of the top teeth in a cross situation. This can lead to problems with the mouth joints and should be treated and intervened as early as possible. We can use an appliance called “palatal expander”. This appliance causes the top jaw to expand and grow thus correcting the crossbite and improving airway as well and it will also reduce the chances of crowding by allowing extra space in the mouth.



  • Thumb sucking: We know it’s cute and comforting for your child to suck the thumb, however this habit can cause severe problems with the top teeth and jaw if not stopped early. The main consequence is an open bite where the front teeth don’t touch. We can produce appliances that could help to discourage your child to continue with the habit.
