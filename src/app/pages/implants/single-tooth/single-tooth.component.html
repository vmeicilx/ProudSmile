Get a second
chance

Sometimes we loose teeth, either from disease or accidents. Fortunately with dental implants we can revert that. Implants are more than just artificial roots and teeth, it’s a second chance to replace vital teeth structure, which are so important to eat, speak, preserve bone and provide aesthetics.

With Implants we can replace 1 or more teeth, or even an entire mouth. They are also very useful to substantially increase stability of dentures. With as little as 2 implants we can stabilise and improve retention on a full lower denture for example.

Also with the help of digital technology, in some situations we can delivery a temporary Implant Crown or Full Arch Prosthesis in as little as 24 hours from surgery. That ensures you’re not toothless during any stage of your treatment. If an immediate Implant crown is not possible we’ll discuss other temporary solutions with you.

“Right”, from the
start

With the advance in digital technology planing an implant surgery has never been more precise and straight forward. By combining a 3D CT x-ray of your mouth with digital scans of the teeth we use advanced software to place an implant completely virtually, all planned with the final outcome in mind. Planning from end to beginning allows more pleasing aesthetics, function and substantially reduces trauma at time of surgery.

From Virtual
to Real

Now the virtual becomes real. That’s done by using a Surgical Guide which is designed in the software and then milled or 3D printed to the exact requirements.

This physical guide then becomes our reference for exact placement of the implants in the mouth, there’s no guess work and no surprises.

Implanted

A stunning smile is no longer only for the genetically blessed individuals. With porcelain veneers anyone(*) can now have a smile to be proud of. Veneers are thin porcelain “shells” that are “bonded” to the front of your teeth and can permanently change the shape and colour of your teeth to your exact requirements. Veneers can give you the smile that you always dreamed of and one that you deserve.

Teeth, Regained

This is the final step and often happens 3 to 6 months after the surgical placement of the implants. A beautiful porcelain crown is made to fit the implant. Again with the use of digital technology, there are no moulds. In the vast majority of cases crowns are screw retained onto the implants which means they are fully retrievable at any point should it ever need repair.

The placement of the crown involves a short 30 minute appointment and it’s also pain free, in some cases no local anaesthetics are even required.

Can anyone get
implants?

There’s no short answer for that. Each case is different and needs to be assessed with care, which is why we can’t often give definitive costings over the phone for instance. However, with the advancements in types and sizes of implants and auxiliary procedures such as bone grafts and the all-on-four protocol there’s a good chance we will be able to find a solution for you. Below are some of the procedures we can do to still be able to place implants even in areas with poor bone availability.

For more complex cases requiring larger interventional procedures we work in conjunction with dental specialists such as oral surgeons and periodontists. These specialists can perform the surgical component and we can continue the case by providing the prosthetics. One of the good advantages of specialists is that surgical procedures can be performed under General Anaesthetics at the hospital.

Bone, the holy
grail for implants

When we talk about implants bone is the single most important factor for successful results. That is because implants actually “fuse” to the bone in the process called Osseointegration. So if for any reason we find that we cannot place an implant due to no bone being available we can perform bone grafts in an attempt to revert the situation. The name Bone Graft can generates anxiety as the public often relates that to very complex and painful procedures but the reality is that some grafting procedures can as simple as placing a granular material inside an extraction socket or into the sinus cavity to allow for new bone to “grow”. The decision on if or when we need a bone grafting will be made usually at the early stages of planning once we look at x-rays and this will be thoroughly explained and discussed with you on your consultation.

So, if youthink you can’t get implants because you may not have enough bone you don’t have to worry. There are still options available to you.

Sinus Lift
test

You’ll probably know what sinuses are if you’ve had a sinusitis before. These are hollow spaces we have on our temple and on the sides of our nose. Our back teeth on the top jaw are often very close to the sinus cavity which then makes implants in these areas more difficult as we’re limited by the floor of the sinus. When you loose back teeth and don’t do anything to replace them the sinus floor moves downwards leaving you with practically no bone left, it’s a process called bone resorption.

The sinus lift procedure allows us to very delicately place bone graft material inside the sinus cavity to allow for formation of new bone which we can then use to place implants. Depending on how much “lift” we need to make we can perform this procedure right in our office under local anaesthesia, it’s completely pain free. Complications such as sinus infection or rupture of the sinus lining can occur but these are minimised with the correct technique and use of digital technology and x-rays and these will be explained to you during your consultation.
